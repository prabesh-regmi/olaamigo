GDPR - Data Protection

Your Rights under the General Data Protection Regulation

Last updated: January 2025

Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and Portuguese Law No. 58/2019 grant you specific rights over your personal data. Three Stars Accounting, Lda. is committed to respecting and facilitating the exercise of these rights.

Our GDPR Commitment

We process your data lawfully, fairly and transparently
We collect data only for specified and legitimate purposes
We limit data to what is strictly necessary
We keep data accurate and up to date
We retain data only for as long as necessary
We ensure security through technical and organizational measures

Your Rights

Right of Access

Art. 15 GDPR

You have the right to obtain confirmation that your data is being processed and to access a copy of it, as well as information about purposes, data categories, recipients and retention periods.

Right to Rectification

Art. 16 GDPR

You can request the correction of inaccurate personal data or the completion of incomplete data concerning you.

Right to Erasure

Art. 17 GDPR

You can request the deletion of your personal data when no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, you object to processing, or data was processed unlawfully.

Right to Restriction

Art. 18 GDPR

You can request restriction of processing while verifying data accuracy, processing lawfulness, or if data is needed for exercising legal rights.

Right to Portability

Art. 20 GDPR

You have the right to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

Right to Object

Art. 21 GDPR

You can object to the processing of your data at any time, on grounds relating to your particular situation, including profiling.

Exceptions and Limitations

Your rights may be limited in certain circumstances, namely:

  • Legal retention obligations (e.g., tax documents for 10 years)
  • Exercise of rights in legal proceedings
  • Important public interest reasons
  • Criminal offense investigations
